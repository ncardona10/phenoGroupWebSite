Phenomenology Group

We explore the intersection between the theory and the experiments. Through simulations we recreate the theoretical behavior of extensions of the Standard Model to find where the experiments should be looking at

VBF Higgsino DM Group

The identity of Dark Matter (DM) is one of the st active topics in particle physics today. Supersymmetric models, theoretical extensions of the Standard Model (SM), provide possible explanations to the particle nature of DM. Considering R-parity conservation in supersymmetric models such as the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) provides a natural DM candidate: the lightest neutralino. Most of the theoretical models have considered the neutralino as a Bino or Wino particle. However, the Higgsino-like neutralino is also an alternate candidate. In the Higgsino search proposed, the Vector Boson Fusion (VBF) topology is used to overcome the limited experimental sensitivity in compressed mass spectra regions. The presentation explores the use of VBF topology to target DM production of Higgsino-like scenarios in compressed mass spectra regions at the LHC.
